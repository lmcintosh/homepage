About Lane McIntosh

I am a graduate student in theoretical neuroscience at Stanford, where I do a mix of theory and experimental tests of theory.

I'm particularly interested in the early stages of how we process vision, where in just three layers of neurons we have a binary code that conveys information about motion, object edges, direction, and even predictions about what will happen next in the world.

I'm also doing a PhD minor in Computer Science, reflecting my belief that our understanding of the most advanced information processing device in the universe - the brain - can inform how we should be building artificially intelligent systems like deep learning networks.
